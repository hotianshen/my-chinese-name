我有嘉名 — “I have a fine name.”

The difference

Not a transliteration. Not fortune-telling.

An offhand sound-alike gives you syllables a stranger will stumble over. A crafted name gives you a person: sound that echoes your own, meaning chosen for who you are, characters that stand upright on the page.

◦  The sound-alike
索菲娅
Suǒ-fēi-yà

Three borrowed syllables, chosen for noise alone — “rope · fragrant · Asia.” No thread of meaning, no image, no roots. A label, not a name.

✦  The crafted name
苏菲
Sū Fēi

For a Sophia: 苏, to revive and to flourish — the surname of Su Dongpo; 菲, fragrant and abundant. Two syllables that keep her sound and give her a spring.

“First, rectify the names.” — Confucius, 正名. A name is the first thing the world learns of you; we make it worthy of the introduction.

承天 · The signature reckoning

Three Powers, Twelve Palaces.

Every name is weighed on one instrument — the 命盘. Heaven, Human, and Earth are the three tiers; within them, twelve palaces test a name from sound to soul.

天
Heaven Tiān · what you reach for

Aspiration, meaning, and image: the character a name calls toward. What you hope to be is written first.

道 · 势 · 格 · 音
人
Human Rén · who you are

Sound, form, and fitness: how the name sits in a mouth, on a page, and beside the person who carries it.

形 · 意 · 象 · 脉

Roots, prudence, and lineage — the surname that grounds you and the red-lines screened, so nothing works against you.

忌 · 愿 · 典 · 命

The twelve: Taiji, Momentum, Stature, Phoenix-Song, Six-Scripts, Meaning, Stellar-River, Lineage, Prudence, Aspiration, Classics, Destiny. Every candidate is scored across all twelve — 命 is but one palace of twelve, never decisive.

A gallery of fine names

Names the world already knows.

The great sinologists did not transliterate themselves — they were named. Each of these is a miniature biography, carried in two or three characters.

费正清 Fèi Zhèngqīng

John King Fairbank · Historian of China

Upright and clear. 正 for rectitude, 清 for clarity — the Harvard scholar's name reads as a scholar's creed. The surname 费 rides the sound of “Fairbank,” and the whole becomes a sentence about a life's work.

何伟 Hé Wěi

Peter Hessler · Writer, The New Yorker

Direct, strong, unmistakably his own. Two clean strokes of sound and character: 何, a common, grounded surname; 伟, greatness worn plainly. A name a Chinese reader trusts before the first sentence.

陆克文 Lù Kèwén

Kevin Rudd · Prime Minister of Australia

Cultured, and easy on the ear. 克文 — “to master the cultured, the literary” — let a Western statesman speak Mandarin on Chinese television and be received as one who had done the reading. A name that opened rooms.

利玛窦 Lì Mǎdòu

Matteo Ricci · 1552–1610 · the root text of all cross-cultural naming

Four centuries ago a Jesuit chose to become 利玛窦 and walked into the Ming court not as a foreigner but as a scholar with a name. That name was not his disguise. It was his bridge. Every name we make descends from his.

名以载道 · The tradition

“A good name is like a lamp: it lights the road a person will walk for a lifetime.”

The Shuowen tells us the character 名 joins “mouth” and “dusk”: a name is how you let someone see you in the dark. Before the light returns, before a face is clear, a name is what you offer across the distance — and what is offered back.

For sixteen centuries, Chinese families have not left this to chance. A name was reasoned: matched in sound, chosen in meaning, shaped by the classical script, and tuned by the ancient scales. We inherit that discipline and extend it to those arriving from another language — the way a host sets a place at the table.

A name is no spell to alter fate; it is a wise light that illuminates one. We do not promise fortune. We promise a name you can stand inside — that fits the person, honours the culture it enters, and holds up under a lifetime of introductions.

A Chinese name chosen with care is a hand you extend to an ancient culture — and the hand that culture extends back to you. This is the whole of our work: to make that handshake true.

May this lamp light your road, too.
