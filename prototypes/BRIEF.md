# Prototype brief — "My Chinese Name" landing page, 5 distinct visual worlds

You are building **one** self-contained landing page (a home page) for **My Chinese
Name** — a premium service that gives English-speaking foreigners an authentic
Chinese name by a 1,600-year scholarly method (the Ho Method: Three Powers &
Twelve Palaces). Companion to a published book by Ho Tianshen.

The bar (non-negotiable, all 5 share it): the visitor's first feeling must be
**"this is an extraordinary, high-taste, trustworthy work of art"** — never a
small-workshop product. Beautiful, philosophically deep copy. Impeccable
typography and spacing. Human-centered, calm, generous — not gamified, no
frivolous clutter. East-meets-West. Museum-grade craft.

## Technical constraints
- **One self-contained `.html` file** (inline `<style>`; Google Fonts via `<link>`
  allowed; inline SVG for art/motifs; a little vanilla JS for tasteful motion is
  fine). Must open correctly as a local file and be fully responsive (down to
  375px). No build step, no frameworks.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Accessible contrast. Semantic HTML.
- Use tasteful, restrained motion (scroll reveals, an ink/seal/stroke moment).
  At most one "hero" animation per viewport.

## Required sections (in this order; you may rename headings in the style's voice)
1. **Top nav** — wordmark "My Chinese Name" + a seal mark; links (The Method,
   Fine Names, Pricing, The Tradition); a language hint (EN · 中文); one primary CTA.
2. **Hero** — the eyebrow "THE HO METHOD · 承天 · THREE POWERS & TWELVE PALACES";
   a large display headline; a philosophical sub-line; the vermilion seal 我有嘉名
   ("I have a fine name"); primary + secondary CTA.
3. **The difference** — "Not a transliteration. Not fortune-telling." Show the
   contrast: an offhand sound-alike (e.g. *Suo-fei-ya*) vs. a crafted name
   (**苏菲 Sū Fēi**). One or two examples.
4. **Three Powers & Twelve Palaces** — 天 (Heaven) · 人 (Human) · 地 (Earth); note
   the twelve palaces within. This is the brand's signature diagram — make it a
   visual centrepiece (a radial 命盘 / three-tier structure).
5. **A gallery of fine names** — real celebrated names, each a miniature
   biography:
   - 费正清 **Fèi Zhèngqīng** — John King Fairbank — "upright and clear"
   - 何伟 **Hé Wěi** — Peter Hessler — "direct, strong, unmistakably his own"
   - 陆克文 **Lù Kèwén** — Kevin Rudd — "cultured, and easy on the ear"
   - 利玛窦 **Lì Mǎdòu** — Matteo Ricci — "the root text of all cross-cultural naming"
6. **The philosophy / the tradition** — one soulful long-form passage (see copy).
7. **Pricing teaser** — Free (a real name) → the Dossier → a Master's name. Keep it elegant, not loud.
8. **Final CTA** — "Your name is waiting to be made." + button.
9. **Footer** — wordmark, tagline, 承天 · 名以载道, links.

## Approved copy (book-accurate — use these; you may lightly adapt to the style's voice)
- Tagline: **More than a translation — a bridge between the name you carry and the culture you meet.**
- Hero headline ideas: *"A Chinese name that is truly yours."* / *"One name. One door."* / *"The name the world will know you by."*
- Hero sub-line: *"Not a transliteration, and not fortune-telling — a name woven from sound, meaning, form, and roots, by a method more than 1,600 years old."*
- The method, in one line: **"Match the sound to your Western name, the meaning to the Eastern spirit; shape the form by the classical script, tune the music by the ancient scales."**
- Luminous lines to weave in (quote sparingly, beautifully):
  - *"A good name is like a lamp: it lights the road a person will walk for a lifetime."*
  - *"A name is no spell to alter fate; it is a wise light that illuminates one."*
  - *"A Chinese name chosen with care is a hand you extend to an ancient culture — and the hand that culture extends back to you."*
  - *"The Shuowen tells us the character 名 joins ‘mouth’ and ‘dusk’: a name is how you let someone see you in the dark."*
  - On Ricci: *"That name was not his disguise. It was his bridge."*
  - Confucius: *"First, rectify the names."* (正名)
  - Closing: *"May this lamp light your road, too."*
- Provenance: a 54th-generation heir; a 1,600-year tradition; the Chengtian studio, Mount Zhongnan; the book on Amazon.
- Seal characters: **我有嘉名** (2×2, read right-to-left top-to-bottom). Brand mark.
- Palette anchor from the book cover: warm rice-paper `#F5F0E6`, ink `#1C1B18`,
  vermilion seal `#C8341F`, gold ochre `#B8923F` — but EACH prototype reinterprets
  colour boldly per its own world (see your style below).

## Craft
Invoke the **impeccable** skill for design guidance before you build. Where a
signature background texture/art would elevate the piece (ink wash, star field,
paper grain, gold hairlines, a seal), generate it — you may use the
**algorithmic-art** or **canvas-design** skills, or hand-write inline SVG.
Polish relentlessly: type scale, optical spacing, letter-spacing on eyebrows,
line-length ≤ 68ch for prose, real hierarchy, no default-looking anything.
