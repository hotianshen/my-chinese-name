名以载道 · A name carries the way

“A good name is like a lamp — it lights the road a person will walk for a lifetime.”

The Shuowen, China's first dictionary, tells us the character 名 joins “mouth” and “dusk”: in the dark, when a face cannot be seen, you speak your name. A name is how you let someone see you in the dark.

So a name is no small thing. It is no spell to alter fate — we make no such promise. It is a wise light that illuminates one. Confucius, asked where he would begin to set the world right, answered plainly: first, rectify the names. 正名.

Four centuries ago a young Jesuit chose to be known as 利玛窦, Lì Mǎdòu. He could have kept his own. He didn't. That name was not his disguise; it was his bridge — the hand he extended to an ancient culture, and the hand that culture extended back.

This is the whole of our craft. Match the sound to your Western name, the meaning to the Eastern spirit; shape the form by the classical script, tune the music by the ancient scales. Then read it against all twelve palaces, and sign it only when it is truly yours.
