The difference

Not a transliteration.
Not fortune-telling.

Most "Chinese names" are a sound spelled out in the nearest characters — an accident, not a choice. A name made by the Ho Method matches the sound to your Western name and the meaning to the Eastern spirit, then shapes the form by the classical script.

Three Powers, Twelve Palaces

The ancients read a life as a small cosmos: 天 Heaven above, 地 Earth below, and 人 the Human between. Every name we make is weighed across twelve palaces within these three — a full reckoning, not a lucky guess.

A gallery of fine names

Names the world already knows

For four centuries, the West's finest China hands carried names made this way — not spelled, but composed. Each is a miniature biography.

费正清
i.
Fèi Zhèngqīng
John King Fairbank · historian of China

The dean of American sinology took a name meaning “upright and clear.” 正 rectifies, 清 clarifies — the whole a quiet manifesto for a life spent making China legible to the West.

何伟
ii.
Hé Wěi
Peter Hessler · writer

Two strokes of plainness: 何 a common surname, 伟 great. Direct, strong, and unmistakably his own — a name that walks into a room without announcing itself, and is remembered anyway.

陆克文
iii.
Lù Kèwén
Kevin Rudd · statesman

A prime minister who could debate in Mandarin wore a name to match: 克文 “able in letters, cultured.” It keeps the sound of “Kevin,” yet lands soft and literate on a Chinese ear.

利玛窦
iv.
Lì Mǎdòu
Matteo Ricci · Jesuit, 1552–1610

The root text of all cross-cultural naming. Four hundred years on, his name still opens doors in Beijing. That name was not his disguise — it was his bridge.

The tradition · 名以载道

A name is no spell to alter fate.
It is a wise light that illuminates one.

The Shuowen, China's first dictionary, tells us the character 名 joins 口 (mouth) and 夕 (dusk): a name is how you let someone see you in the dark. When faces fade at nightfall, the name is what you call across the distance — and what answers.

So a good name is like a lamp: it lights the road a person will walk for a lifetime. Ours is not chosen by chance or by machine, but reckoned — sound matched to your Western name, meaning to the Eastern spirit, form shaped by the classical script, music tuned to the ancient scales.

This is the inheritance of a single line — a 54th-generation heir to a naming tradition more than 1,600 years old, kept at the Chengtian studio beneath Mount Zhongnan. The method is written down; the judgment is handed, teacher to student, across the centuries.
